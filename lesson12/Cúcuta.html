Cúcuta is a Colombian city on the border with Venezuela, known for its military history. The Monument to the Battle of Cúcuta marks the point where military leader Simón Bolívar is believed to have assembled his troops in 1813. The Malecón is a coastal walkway along the river. The Clock Tower building has art exhibits. Cerro Cristo Rey has a statue of Christ and views of the city. The museum of the birthplace of General Santander is in Villa del Rosario, to the south.

Economy:

The municipality is located in one of the richest regions of the state, responsible for approximately 8.5% of its gross domestic product. Within a radius of one hundred kilometers from Jaboticabal, there are 83 municipalities that have their own tax revenue in excess of twenty million dollars, housing more than 2.8 million inhabitants.
Cúcuta also stands out as the largest producer of ceramic artifacts in Colombia, focused almost exclusively on the production of filters and vases, mostly made by hand, molded by hand, on wooden lathes. The municipality also has the highest peanut productivity in Colombia.

Come to Cúcuta, enjoy its beautiful climate, its high quality of life and public safety. In Jaboticabal you will find friendly and highly qualified people, who will help you get your business off the ground and reach the heights.
